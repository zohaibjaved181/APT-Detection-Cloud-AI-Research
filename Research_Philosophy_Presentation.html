Research Methodology Philosophy & Approach

Detection of Advanced Persistent Threats in Cloud Networks Using Artificial Intelligence

This research investigates the application of AI and machine learning techniques to detect sophisticated, multi-stage Advanced Persistent Threats (APTs) targeting cloud computing infrastructure. The study aims to develop a novel, explainable detection framework that addresses gaps in real-time processing, adversarial robustness, and cross-stage threat correlation.

Research Focus: Empirical evaluation of AI models on cloud network data, measuring detection accuracy, false positive rates, inference latency, and explainability of threat classifications across multi-cloud environments.

Epistemology

Research Philosophies

An examination of four major research philosophies and their applicability to AI-based APT detection research

🔬

Positivism

Knowledge through observable, measurable facts. Emphasizes objectivity, quantification, and hypothesis testing.

STRENGTHS

Enables quantitative evaluation of detection accuracy, precision, recall. Supports reproducible experiments on benchmark datasets.

LIMITATIONS

May overlook contextual factors of APT behavior. Cannot capture the evolving, adaptive nature of threat actors.

💬

Interpretivism

Understanding through subjective experiences and meanings. Focuses on context, interpretation, and qualitative insight.

STRENGTHS

Useful for understanding SOC analyst decision-making and how humans interpret AI-generated threat alerts.

LIMITATIONS

Not ideal for validating AI model performance. Lacks generalizability needed for system-level threat detection.

⚙

Pragmatism

Knowledge judged by practical consequences. Supports mixed methods — whatever works best for the research problem.

STRENGTHS

Allows combining quantitative model metrics with qualitative expert validation. Flexible and problem-oriented.

LIMITATIONS

May lack philosophical rigour. Risk of inconsistency when mixing paradigms without clear justification.

🌍

Realism

Reality exists independently of observation. Accepts both observable data and underlying causal mechanisms.

STRENGTHS

Acknowledges complex, layered nature of APTs. Supports exploring causal mechanisms behind detection success/failure.

LIMITATIONS

Harder to operationalize in purely computational experiments. May require broader research scope.

Selected Philosophy

Positivism — The Most Suitable Philosophy

Positivism is selected as the guiding research philosophy for this study on AI-based APT detection in cloud networks

🎯 Why Positivism?

✓
Research relies on quantitative, measurable outcomes — accuracy, precision, recall, F1-score, inference time
✓
Requires objective, reproducible experiments using standardized datasets and controlled conditions
✓
Involves hypothesis testing — e.g., "AI model X detects APTs with significantly higher accuracy than baseline"
✓
Supports generalizability of findings across different cloud environments and attack scenarios
✓
Aligns with scientific method dominant in computer science and cybersecurity research

📊 Alignment with Research Problem

The research problem demands:

  • Empirical validation of AI model performance on network traffic data
  • Statistical comparison of multiple detection algorithms
  • Controlled experiments with training/testing splits, cross-validation
  • Value-free observation — system performance is objective, not subjective
  • Causal relationships — identifying which features/architectures cause better detection

Key Insight: Since APT detection is fundamentally a classification/anomaly detection problem evaluated through numerical metrics, positivism provides the most rigorous framework for validating our AI approach.

Research Approach

Deductive Approach — Theory to Evidence

A deductive research approach is adopted, moving from established theories and hypotheses to empirical testing and validation

🔄 Deductive vs Inductive

DEDUCTIVE ✓

Theory → Hypothesis → Data Collection → Testing → Confirmation/Rejection

INDUCTIVE ✗

Observation → Pattern → Hypothesis → Theory (exploratory, less suitable here)

🧪 Deductive Process for This Study

1. Theory: AI/DL can model complex, non-linear attack patterns
⬇
2. Hypothesis: Proposed hybrid model achieves >95% detection rate
⬇
3. Experiment: Train/test on cloud APT datasets with baselines
⬇
4. Validate: Statistical tests confirm/reject hypothesis

✅ Why Deductive is Suitable

1
Existing theoretical foundation: Established ML/DL theories on pattern recognition and anomaly detection provide clear starting hypotheses
2
Testable predictions: We can formulate specific, falsifiable hypotheses about model performance metrics
3
Structured methodology: Allows systematic comparison of proposed vs. existing approaches
4
Generalizability: Results can be applied beyond the specific test dataset to broader cloud environments
5
Alignment with positivism: Deductive approach naturally complements the positivist philosophy selected
6
Benchmarking standard: Consistent with how ML/AI research is conducted and peer-reviewed in top venues

Final Justification

How Philosophy & Approach Support the Research

Demonstrating coherent alignment between positivism + deductive approach and research objectives, data collection, and analysis

🎯 Supporting Research Objectives

Objective 1: Develop AI detection framework
→ Positivism enables objective measurement of framework performance
Objective 2: Compare with existing methods
→ Deductive approach structures hypothesis-driven comparison
Objective 3: Validate in cloud environments
→ Positivism demands empirical testing in realistic scenarios
Objective 4: Ensure explainability
→ Measurable XAI metrics (fidelity, stability) align with positivist evaluation

📂 Supporting Data Collection

• Quantitative network traffic datasets (CICIDS, Unraveled, DARPA)
• Structured numerical features extracted from cloud logs & flows
• Controlled experimental conditions with train/validation/test splits
• Reproducible simulations using MITRE ATT&CK attack scenarios

📊 Supporting Data Analysis

• Statistical hypothesis testing (t-tests, ANOVA, Wilcoxon)
• Performance metrics — Accuracy, Precision, Recall, F1, AUC-ROC
• Confusion matrices & ROC curves for visual validation
• Cross-validation (k-fold) ensuring robustness and generalizability

Conclusion: The combination of Positivism (philosophy) and Deductive Approach provides a rigorous, objective, and systematic framework perfectly suited to developing, testing, and validating an AI-based APT detection system through empirical experimentation and statistical analysis.
