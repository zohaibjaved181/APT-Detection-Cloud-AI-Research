📋 Activity 1: Research Methodology Selection

Identifying the Right Research Methodology

Our study focuses on detecting Advanced Persistent Threats in cloud networks using artificial intelligence. Before diving into experiments, we need to select a methodology that matches the nature of our research problem and the kind of evidence we want to produce.

Background & Problem Statement

Cloud computing has become the backbone of modern enterprise infrastructure, but it has also opened new doors for sophisticated attackers. Advanced Persistent Threats are long-running, carefully planned campaigns that infiltrate networks and stay hidden for months while extracting sensitive data.

The core problem is straightforward: traditional security tools cannot keep up with these threats. They rely on known signatures and simple rules, which APT actors easily bypass. We need intelligent systems that can learn patterns and adapt.

Research Aim & Objectives

Aim: To develop and evaluate an AI-driven detection framework capable of identifying multi-stage APT attacks in cloud network environments.

  • 1Design a hybrid deep learning architecture for cloud traffic analysis
  • 2Benchmark performance against established detection methods
  • 3Evaluate real-time detection feasibility in dynamic cloud environments
  • 4Provide explainable outputs that security teams can act on

Significance: This work contributes a practical, validated AI framework that addresses documented gaps in cloud-specific APT detection, benefiting both the research community and industry practitioners.

🧭 Research Philosophy & Approach

Evaluating Research Philosophies

Every research project rests on philosophical assumptions about how knowledge is created. We evaluate four major philosophies below to determine which one best fits our work.

🔬 Positivism

Holds that reliable knowledge comes from observable, measurable phenomena. Values objectivity, hypothesis testing, and statistical verification. The researcher remains detached from the subject being studied.

STRENGTHS FOR OUR TOPIC

Perfectly suits our need to measure detection accuracy, compare algorithms through metrics, and produce reproducible results. Our AI models generate numerical outputs that demand statistical analysis.

LIMITATIONS

Cannot capture the strategic reasoning of human threat actors or the contextual nuances of security decision-making. Treats the detection problem purely as a numbers game.

💬 Interpretivism

Argues that knowledge is constructed through subjective human experience. Emphasises understanding meanings, motivations, and context rather than counting occurrences.

STRENGTHS FOR OUR TOPIC

Could help explore how security analysts interpret threat alerts or how organisational culture affects incident response. Valuable for human-centred security research.

LIMITATIONS

Our research question is not about human perceptions — it is about whether an AI system can detect attacks effectively. Subjective interpretation does not help us validate model accuracy.

⚙ Pragmatism

Takes the position that the value of knowledge depends on its practical usefulness. Supports mixing methods — whatever approach best answers the research question is acceptable.

STRENGTHS FOR OUR TOPIC

Allows flexibility. We could combine quantitative experiments with qualitative expert feedback to get a richer picture of system effectiveness.

LIMITATIONS

For our specific study, the additional qualitative layer adds complexity without proportionate benefit. Our primary question is answerable through numbers alone.

🌍 Realism

Maintains that an objective reality exists independent of our observations, but acknowledges we can only partially access it. Distinguishes between what we observe and underlying mechanisms.

STRENGTHS FOR OUR TOPIC

Recognises that APT behaviour has underlying causal structures we may not fully observe. Supports investigating why certain models work better than others.

LIMITATIONS

More suited to explanatory social research. Harder to operationalise cleanly in a computational study focused on model benchmarking.

Our Choice: Positivism — Because our research revolves around measurable model performance, controlled experiments, and statistical hypothesis testing, positivism provides the most coherent philosophical foundation. It aligns naturally with the deductive, quantitative nature of AI systems research.

📊 Methodology Selection

Quantitative Methodology: The Natural Choice

Given our positivist stance and the computational nature of our research, a quantitative methodology is the most appropriate path forward. Here is why the alternatives do not fit as well.

📝

Qualitative

Explores meanings and experiences through interviews, focus groups, or observations. Good for understanding the "why" behind human behaviour.

Not suitable — Our research produces numerical model outputs and network metrics. We are not investigating human perceptions or social phenomena.

📊

Quantitative

SELECTED

Relies on measurable data, statistical analysis, and controlled experiments. Tests specific hypotheses and produces generalisable results.

Perfect fit — Everything about our study is quantifiable: detection rates, processing times, accuracy scores, false positive counts.

🔀

Mixed Methods

Combines quantitative and qualitative approaches for a broader perspective. Useful when research questions span both numerical and experiential domains.

Unnecessary — Would add complexity without proportional gain. Our questions are entirely answerable through computational experiments and statistical evidence.

Why Quantitative Works for Us

  • ✓We train machine learning models on numerical datasets containing millions of network flow records
  • ✓Success is measured through concrete metrics — accuracy, precision, recall, F1-score, AUC-ROC
  • ✓We use controlled experiments with training/testing splits and cross-validation protocols
  • ✓Conclusions rest on statistical significance tests rather than subjective judgement

The connection between our choices is straightforward:

Each element reinforces the others, creating a logically consistent research design suited to answering our core question with confidence.
